▶ 📋 Model Assumptions & Calibration Notes v3.2 — click to expand 🔧 v3.2 Bug Fixes (policy-grade accuracy improvements)
Gini with negative wealth

Fix: Gini now computed on wealth clamped to ≥0. Prior formula (2·Σi·wᵢ)/(n·W)−(n+1)/n produced values outside [0,1] when WEALTH_FLOOR (−$10K) generated negative wᵢ. Standard approach for wealth Gini with negative holdings.

agentEDC income proxy

Fix: agentEDC now uses actual agent wage (matching v3.1 BLEI fix). Previously used wealth/60 proxy — a different income estimate than BLEI — creating internal welfare-measure inconsistency. EDC and BLEI now share the same income denominator.

lognormal() numerical stability

Fix: Box-Muller uses Math.max(1e-14, 1−RNG()). If RNG()=1 (possible edge), prior code set u=0 giving log(0)=−Infinity, producing NaN wealth/wage for a fraction of agents in some seeds.

gamma() infinite loop & comparison RNG

Fix 1: gamma() rejection sampling capped at 5,000 iterations with fallback return. Fix 2: CCO comparison agents now use deterministic participation assignment (first 78% by index), not RNG()<0.78 — which consumed RNG state before the main run, undermining seed-based reproducibility.

📊 Empirical Inputs — sourced from real-world data
Daily Living Costs

Base daily cost $68.33/day and CCO+PTH daily cost $31.67/day — US Bureau of Labor Statistics Consumer Expenditure Survey 2023. Components: rent/PTH + food + utilities + transport, divided by 30 days.

EDC Near-Poverty Baseline & Gini

62–72% extractive drain coefficient for near-poverty US households — US CES 2023 rent-to-income and consumer debt ratios. Nordic Gini benchmark 0.28 — OECD 2023.

Agent Wealth Distribution

Starting wealth lognormal(10.5, 1.2) calibrated loosely against Fed SCF 2022 net-worth distribution for non-homeowners (median ≈ $36K). PTF ε range 1.50–3.00 grounded in Mondragon Eroski cooperative pricing data (Whyte & Whyte 1991). Baseline agents start at wealth×0.85 — representing lower accumulated savings in the absence of CCO stabilisation.

Acre Equity Appreciation

r_a = 4%/yr base (range 2–7%). Calibrated against Community Land Trust long-run data (Davis & Demetrowitz 2003; Theodos et al. 2017 — 70% lower foreclosure rate vs. conventional). PTH payment 100% generative (zero EDC), consistent with Burlington CLT documentation.

⚖️ Calibration Targets — framework-specified; subject to revision
Annual Base Cost & Unit System

ANNUAL_BASE_COST ($1,500) calibrated to simulation unit scale, not directly US CES ($60–72K BLS 2023). Operative ratio: wage×12/ANNUAL_BASE_COST <1 for baseline (wealth depletion), >1 for CCO participants (accumulation). Wages in Simulation Income Units (SIU), not USD.

PTF Share, Target Wealth & Other Targets

PTF 18% share and target wealth $82K are framework specifications under active revision. An open goal of this simulation is to identify optimal CCO parameters — analogous to a structural engineer ensuring load capacity across all points simultaneously.

Flourishing Threshold (730 days)

Two full years of basic living covered — capital commitment horizon required for entrepreneurial ventures to be viable without first-year break-even (BLEI §4). Requires CCO+PTF both active; without them top tier displays as "Comfortable."

Poverty Measures

Wealth poverty line $25,000. BLEI poverty (% agents with BLEI < 30 days = Crisis+Precarious) is more theoretically grounded — a retiree with $24K savings and $80K pension registers as wealth-poor but correctly as BLEI-stable. Both shown for multi-dimensional assessment.

🔬 Theoretical Mechanisms — ABM design choices
BLEI Formula (three components)

BLEI = cashDays + incomeDays + benefitDays = (liquid + γ·wage + buFood) / dailyCost. v3.2 Distribution Trajectory charts decompose these three components separately over time. γ = 0.12 baseline → 0.20 with established CCO floor.

Wage Dynamics & AI Automation

Wages grow: 1% base + 0.8% BLEI stability premium + 0.3%/octave + CIP bonus. AI automation: each agent has automationRisk ∈ [0.2,1.0] — displacement = populationRate × automationRisk. Wage floor: max(0.80×, growth-adjusted). Annual wage floor (20% drop limit) prevents runaway collapse; documented as policy assumption.

Cost Structure, PTF & PTH

PTF modelled as cost reduction factor (not wealth injection — v3.0 fix). PTH covers housing component (35%) and builds Acre Equity separately — no double-counting. CCO food basket reduces food component (20%). PTF dynamic adoption: agents join via economic distress or diffusion (v3.1).

Recession, Debt & FBS Gating

Recessions: population-level, 10% annual probability, 2–4 year duration, incomeMultiplier 0.65–0.85. Agents can accumulate debt to −$10,000 floor. FBS gating per Mullainathan & Shafir (2013): advancement infeasible at FBS ≈ 0.
