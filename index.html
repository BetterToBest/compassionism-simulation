▶ 📋 Model Assumptions & Calibration Notes v3.0 — click to expand
Wealth & Expense Dynamics

Agents earn wage income offset by annual living costs (v3.0: $1,500/yr base). PTF reduces costs directly rather than adding wealth. PTH covers the housing component (35% of costs) and builds Acre Equity separately — these are no longer double-counted. CCO food basket covers a further 20% of costs. Under baseline with no CCO, wages alone cannot cover living costs over the long run, creating realistic wealth depletion and poverty dynamics. Wealth floor at $100.

Wage Dynamics (v3.0)

Wages initialised from lognormal(μ=3.5, σ=0.5). Annual growth: 1% base + 0.8% when above Precarious BLEI tier (stability premium) + 0.3% per octave level (skill premium) + CIP access bonus. When AI automation is enabled, progressive displacement begins at year 5 (≈2030) at 1.2%/yr, accelerating at year 15 (≈2040) to 2.2%/yr — beyond which labour markets cannot reabsorb at the required rate.

Recession & Shock Model (v3.0)

Recessions are now population-level events (10% annual probability) persisting 2–4 years at 65–85% income severity. This replaces the v2.x per-agent shock which only modelled transient individual disruptions, not the persistent unemployment and wealth destruction of real recessions. Individual agents retain idiosyncratic income variance around the population-level shock.

Inflation Mechanism (v3.0)

Annual costs compound at the set inflation rate (0–6%). PTF presence dampens effective inflation (community ownership reduces price volatility by up to 50% of the PTF market share); PTH dampens housing-component inflation (10% reduction). Real wages erode when inflation exceeds wage growth. The US Baseline preset applies 3% to reflect historical CPI. Compassionism presets default to 0% (price-stable outcome hypothesis).

BLEI Formula & Tier Naming (v3.0)

BLEI = (liquid + γ·wage + buFood) / dailyCost. v3.0 fix: income buffer uses actual agent wage, not wealth/60 proxy (audit recommendation; wealth ≠ income for debtors/renters). Top tier: Flourishing when CCO+PTF are both active (maturing generative effects); Comfortable without CCO or PTF (BLEI §4 naming convention, Duke's note).

Calibration Sources

Daily basic cost $68.33 — US CES 2023. CCO+PTH daily cost $31.67 — BLEI §3.2. EDC near-poverty baseline 62–72% — US CES 2023. Nordic Gini benchmark 0.28 — OECD. PTF optimal share 18% — framework spec. Flourishing ≥730 days — 2-year entrepreneurial capital commitment horizon (BLEI §4). Many coefficients are calibrated rather than empirically estimated; see CONTRIBUTING.md for validation opportunities.
